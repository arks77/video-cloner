# Problem → solution (before / after)

**Use when:** the classic direct-response arc — show a relatable **pain**, introduce the
product, show the **relief / after**. The highest-converting structure for performance ads.

**Backend rules:** comply with `adapters/active.md`. Every prompt = ONE max-length clip.
Usually has VO/dialogue → the **dialogue gate applies**.

## What defines this style

Contrast. The whole spot lives on the gap between *before* and *after*. The before beat must
be specifically, recognizably annoying (not vague) so the viewer thinks "that's me"; the
after beat must show the pain **resolved on screen**, not just claimed. The product is the
hinge between them. Honest, slightly dramatized frustration in the first beat is what earns
attention; the visible relief is what earns the click.

## The structure (4 layers)

1. **Pain** — the specific, relatable problem, shown physically.
2. **Turn** — the product enters as the fix.
3. **Relief** — the after state, the problem visibly gone.
4. **CTA** — the short call to action.

## Layer-by-layer formula

### Layer 1: Pain
**Pattern:** `{{PERSON}} dealing with {{SPECIFIC_PROBLEM}}, {{FRUSTRATION_BEAT}}.`
Be concrete: not "tired", but "hits snooze for the fourth time, groaning into the pillow".

### Layer 2: Turn
**Pattern:** `{{TRANSITION}} — they reach for {{PRODUCT}}, {{INTRO_LINE}}.`

### Layer 3: Relief
**Pattern:** `{{TRANSITION}} — {{AFTER_STATE}}, the problem visibly resolved.`
Show the change (posture, expression, result), don't just say it improved.

### Layer 4: CTA
**Pattern:** `"{{CTA_LINE}}"`

## Beat structure (single clip)

| Beat | Content |
|------|---------|
| BEFORE | the specific pain, dramatized, relatable |
| PRODUCT | the turn — product enters as the fix |
| AFTER | visible relief + short CTA |

## Multi-clip strategy

Split when the before needs room: clip 1 = pure pain (no product), clip 2 = product + the
shift, clip 3 = after + CTA. Keep the same person and setting so the before/after reads.

## Tone & pacing guide

Before beat = slightly heavy/frustrated; after beat = lighter, exhaled. The energy *lifts*
across the clip. Pacing cue: `frustrated and weary at first, then noticeably lighter after`.

## Complete template

```
[00:00] BEFORE — {{PERSON}} dealing with {{SPECIFIC_PROBLEM}}, {{FRUSTRATION_BEAT}}: "{{PAIN_LINE}}"
[00:0X] PRODUCT — {{TRANSITION}}, reaches for {{PRODUCT}}: "{{INTRO_LINE}}"
[00:0Y] AFTER — {{AFTER_STATE}}, the problem visibly resolved: "{{CTA_LINE}}"
The energy lifts across the clip — frustrated at first, noticeably lighter after.
{{LOOK_AND_DEVICE}}.
```

## Example (a tangle-free charging cable — not the source content)

```
[00:00] BEFORE — a woman at her desk yanks a knotted charging cable, it won't reach the
outlet, she sighs hard: "Every single morning with this thing."
[00:05] PRODUCT — quick cut, she picks up a flat magnetic cable: "Okay, last try."
[00:09] AFTER — the cable snaps to her phone, clean and slack-free, she leans back and
smiles: "That's it. Never going back." Tap to grab one.
The energy lifts across the clip — frustrated at first, noticeably lighter after. Bright
home-office light, phone-quality handheld.
```
